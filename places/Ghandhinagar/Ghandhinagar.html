Gandhinagar is the capital of the state of Gujarat. Gandhinagar is located approximately 23 km north of Ahmedabad, on the west central point of the Industrial corridor between Delhi, the political capital of India, the financial capital of India

About Ghandhinagar

Gandhinagar is a planned city in the western Indian state of Gujarat. The Akshardham is a massive Hindu temple with ornate carvings and sculpted pillars, plus a water show in its sprawling gardens. Dandi Kutir museum traces the life of leader Mahatma Gandhi, who was born in Gujarat. South, along the Sabarmati River, the Indroda Nature Park has a zoo and botanical gardens, plus an adjacent dinosaur and fossil park.

Best time to visit

Gandhinagar prepares for the monsoon season. The months of June to September are characterised by heavy rainfall, enhancing the beauty of the city even further. The monsoon season ensures lush greenery all around and is the best time to enjoy Gandhinagar in its rain-washed splendor. If you decide to visit Gandhinagar in the monsoons do make sure you carry an umbrella or raincoat along.
